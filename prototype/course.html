ELA Core 8th grade

Course Overview

The Grade 8 Core ELA course is the final stage in the middle school progression (Self → Belonging → Systems), preparing students to analyze complex literature and historical narratives through the lens of power, justice, and societal systems.


This course challenges students to move beyond individual identity to confront the nature of oppression, cultural memory, and the role of storytelling in imagining a more just future. Students will engage with essential questions that span history and genre, asking: How do we use stories to remember, resist, and rebuild?

Key Focus

Analyzing how authors use narrative forms (graphic memoir, dystopian fiction, verse novel) to expose systemic injustice, preserve cultural memory, and establish agency in the face of power.

Arcs of inquiry

The Grade 8 Core ELA course uses arcs of inquiry to help students analyze literature and historical narratives through the lens of power, justice, and societal systems.
